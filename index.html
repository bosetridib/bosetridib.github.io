Abstract

Currently a Subject Matter Expert in Economics, I graduated from Banaras Hindu University, India. During my master's degree (MA-Economics), I chose econometrics as a specialization. My research area is Time-Series Econometrics, and I completed my master's dissertation in application of Granger-Causality on Indian macroeconomic variable.

I am currently in pursuit of improving my research skills. I have completed several online courses in R, advanced time-series econometrics and data-science concepts. I have developed independent projects in volatility and covid-19 analysis.

My covid-19 project includes estimating the daily-cases curve with probability distribution functions, and forecasting with those estimates. In my volatility project, I tried to explain volatility by a fluctuating trend, rather than a fluctuating variance. I used Weierstrass function to in the model with different number of terms to create volatility in the simulated data, and then re-estimate the data. Afterwards, I attempted to apply it on the INR/USD exchange rate.

I am profient in R and Python, and I intend to use data-science tools in my research. The econometrics and data-science methods are gradually merging, and I want to begin my research career in this area.
